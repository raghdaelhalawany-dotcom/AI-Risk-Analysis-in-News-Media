üìä Project Overview

This project analyzes how artificial intelligence risks are portrayed in mainstream media, using The New York Times as a case study. By combining advanced NLP techniques, LLM-based classification, and temporal analysis, I mapped the evolution and framing of AI risk narratives from 2012 to 2024.

Research Question:

How has media coverage of AI risks evolved over time, and what patterns emerge in the framing of different risk categories?

Major Insights

  • Societal & Economic Risks dominate the conversation, appearing in 40% of AI-related articles
  • Governance & Regulatory concerns showed the fastest growth rate post-ChatGPT launch
  • Existential risk narratives decreased in relative frequency after 2023, replaced by immediate practical concerns
  • Network analysis revealed strong co-occurrence between privacy, discrimination, and governance issues
  • Sentiment volatility highest for safety and technical failure categories, indicating ongoing debate

3. Multi-Dimensional Analysis

Implemented several analytical approaches to uncover patterns:

  • Frequency Analysis: Identified most discussed risk categories
  • Sentiment Analysis: Measured emotional tone and controversy levels
  • Temporal Analysis: Tracked risk velocity and narrative acceleration
  • Network Analysis: Mapped co-occurrence patterns between risk categories
  • Domain Clustering: Grouped risks into theoretical frameworks

4. Theoretical Framework

Developed a risk domain taxonomy grouping 20 categories into 6 meta-domains:

Risk Domain Hierarchy:
  • Societal & Economic Risks - Job displacement, inequality, economic disruption
  • Governance & Regulatory Risks - Policy gaps, accountability, liability
  • Discrimination, Fairness & Social Harms - Bias, privacy, vulnerable populations
  • Safety, Reliability & Technical Failure - System errors, dataset quality, healthcare risks
  • Malicious Use & Security Threats - Cybersecurity, copyright infringement
  • Long-term / Transformative Risks - Existential concerns, AGI scenarios
